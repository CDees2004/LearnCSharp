Introduction to Computers: 
	Never write your own program if there is already one available. 

Data and Information: 
	Data is the collection of ons and offs which computers store
	and manipulate. 
	
	Information is the interpretation of the data by people 
	to mean something. 
	
	Computers process data and humans work on information. 
	
	A computer does not "know" what the data it is processing 
	actually means. As far as the computer is concerned, it 
	is just a pattern of bits. Users give meaning to these 
	patterns. 

Data Processing: 
	Computers are data processors. Information is fed into 
	them; they do something with it, and then generate further
	information. A computer program tells the computer what 
	to do with the information coming in. 
	
	A computer takes in data, something is performed, then 
	the computer returns data. Possibly altered. 
	
At the bottom there is always hardware: 
	You must make sure that the code you write will actually 
	fit in the target machine and operate at a reasonable 
	speed.
